# Warping Woods — Instruction Booklet

---

## 1. Overview

**Warping Woods** is a cooperative campaign board game for 1–5 players. All 5 characters are always in play — when playing with fewer than 5 players, one or more players control multiple characters. The group must defeat **Bill the Warping Wizard** before the entire world is warped beyond repair.

- **Players:** 1–5
- **Characters:** 5 (always all in play)
- **Rounds:** 24
- **Warp Events:** 3 (end of rounds 6, 12, and 18)
- **Goal:** Defeat Bill before the end of round 24

## 4. The Map

### 4.1 Layout

The map is a **4×4 grid of 3×3 blocks**, totalling **144 tiles**.

### 4.2 Block Types

**Special Blocks (3):**

| Block | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Summoning Block** | Starting location for all characters. Defeated characters are revived here. |
| **Shop Block** | Purchase items using collected resources. |
| **Warping Wizard Block** | Bill's location. The site of the final encounter. |

**Standard Blocks (13):**

Each standard block contains:
- **1 Encounter Tile** — triggers an encounter card when a character lands on it
- **6 Walkable Tiles** — normal movement tiles
- **2 Unwalkable Tiles** — impassable terrain

The arrangement of tiles within each block is random and unique per block.

## 5. Warping

### 5.1 When Warping Occurs

Warping happens at the **end of rounds 6, 12, and 18**. Round 24 is the game-end (no warp, but Bill must be defeated by then).

### 5.2 What Happens During a Warp

All blocks that have **no characters standing on them** are:
1. **Shuffled** to new positions on the 4×4 grid
2. **Randomly rotated** (90°, 180°, 270°, or 360°)
3. **Encounter tokens reset** on warped blocks only

### 5.3 Block Shielding

A character standing on a block **shields** it from warping:
- The block is not moved or rotated
- Encounter tokens on that block are not reset
- Any active combat or quest on that block persists

This means players must coordinate to protect quest progress and active encounters.

## 6. Turn Structure

Each round, every character takes a turn (order determined by player agreement or a fixed sequence).

### 6.1 On Your Turn

1. **Move** — Move up to your Speed in steps (base 4)
2. **Encounter** — If you land on an encounter tile, draw an encounter card and resolve it
3. **Combat** — If the encounter is hostile, you enter combat immediately; your speed drops to 0
4. **Continue / Escape** — If already in combat at the start of your turn, you may continue combat or attempt to escape

### 6.2 Movement Rules

- You cannot move while in combat
- You may not move through unwalkable tiles
- You may freely move through tiles occupied by other characters

### 6.3 Actions per Turn

| Action | Available? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Move | Yes | Up to Speed steps |

## 7. Encounters

### 7.1 Drawing Encounters

When a character lands on an **encounter tile**, they draw the top card from the **encounter deck** and resolve it immediately.

### 7.2 Neutral Encounters

Neutral encounters involve meeting villagers, healers, or other non-hostile characters. They may offer:
- **Immediate rewards** — healing, stat boosts, items
- **Quests** — given at tile (X, Y); the player must return to the original encounter tile to resolve the quest and receive rewards

### 7.3 Hostile Encounters

Hostile encounters are **Warped Creatures** that must be defeated in combat. Upon defeating a creature:
- Draw a **treasure card** for loot
- You may keep the **monster card** if it contains a quest item

### 7.4 Encounter Tile Deactivation

After an encounter is resolved, the encounter tile is **flipped face-down** and deactivated. It remains inactive until the next warp resets it.

### 7.5 Quests

- A quest is triggered at a specific tile (X, Y) via a neutral encounter
- The player must **return to that tile** to resolve the quest and collect rewards
- If the block containing the quest tile is warped (no character on it during a warp), the quest is **reset and lost**

## 8. Combat

Combat is a **turn-based dice system**.

### 8.1 Starting Combat

When a hostile encounter is drawn, combat begins immediately. The character's speed drops to **0** for the remainder of the turn.

### 8.2 Combat Turn

On each subsequent turn while in combat:

1. **Player acts first:**
   - Roll attack dice (Attack stat modifies the roll)
   - Spend AP to use special abilities (optional)
2. **Enemy acts:**
   - The Warped Creature attacks back
   - Damage is reduced by the character's Defence / Armor
3. **Check for defeat:**
   - If the creature's HP reaches 0 → combat ends, loot is gained
   - If the character's HP reaches 0 → character is defeated, sent to Summoning Block

### 8.3 Escaping Combat

A character may attempt to **escape** on their turn by making a **"run away" roll**:
- Success: Character disengages and may move normally on their next turn
- Failure: Combat continues as normal

## 11. Revival

### 11.1 Defeated Characters

When a character's HP reaches 0, they are **defeated** and sent to the **Summoning Block**.

### 11.2 Resummoning

- Another player must have a character **standing on a tile within the Summoning Block**
- The resummoning player's turn **ends immediately** upon resummoning
- The revived character **does not act this round** — they begin their turn normally in the **next round**
